How the Fly Finds Food

FlySim · connectome sensorimotor loop

The fly doesn't know where the food is. It smells its way there — and that makes a real closed feedback loop: its antennae sense the odor, the brain decides which way is stronger, the legs carry it that way, and moving changes what it smells… which feeds straight back in. Once it arrives and tastes sugar, a second, hard-wired reflex fires the tongue. ● smell drives the search ● taste drives feeding ● motor neurons act

Honest footnote. This is the brain-only connectome (139,266 neurons / 16.8M synapses) — the ventral nerve cord that drives the legs isn't in the dataset. So the sensing is real neuron firing (the olfactory ORNs genuinely fire by odor concentration; sugar GRNs → MN9 → proboscis is the validated pathway), while the walking itself is a behavioural layer driven by that olfactory signal. Smell finds the food; the connectome decides to eat.
